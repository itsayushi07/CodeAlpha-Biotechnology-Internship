# Medical Biotechnology Review (2020–2025)

## Overview
This repository contains a structured literature review on recent advancements in medical biotechnology, focusing on vaccines, diagnostics, and stem cell therapy.

The work analyzes emerging trends from 2020–2025, highlighting technological innovations, clinical applications, and translational challenges.

---

## Key Focus Areas

### 1. Vaccine Biotechnology
- mRNA vaccine platforms and rapid development systems  
- Self-amplifying RNA (saRNA) technologies  
- AI-driven vaccine design and antigen prediction  
- Personalized cancer vaccines  

---

### 2. Diagnostic Biotechnology
- Molecular diagnostics (qPCR, dPCR, multiplex assays)  
- CRISPR-based diagnostic systems (SHERLOCK, DETECTR)  
- AI-integrated diagnostic models  
- Point-of-care and decentralized diagnostic technologies  
- Wearable health monitoring systems  

---

### 3. Stem Cell Therapy
- Embryonic, adult, and induced pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs)  
- Gene-edited stem cell applications  
- Stem cell-derived exosome therapies  
- Organoid and tissue engineering models  
- Precision regenerative medicine approaches  

---

## Technological Convergence
Modern biotechnology is increasingly defined by the integration of:

- Artificial Intelligence in diagnostics and vaccine design  
- Gene editing in regenerative medicine  
- Multi-omics data for precision healthcare  
- Computational biology in predictive modeling  

---

## Key Challenges
- Scalability of personalized therapies  
- High cost of advanced biomedical technologies  
- Regulatory and clinical translation barriers  

---

## Future Perspectives
- AI-driven drug and vaccine development  
- Personalized and predictive medicine systems  
- In vivo cellular reprogramming technologies  
- Integration of digital health ecosystems with biotechnology  

---

## Conclusion
Medical biotechnology is transitioning toward predictive, preventive, and personalized healthcare systems. While innovation is rapid, clinical translation and accessibility remain major challenges.
